Singapore heritage

Haji Lane

From quaint cafes to amazing murals, this lane provides everything for a leisure time. Even the crowd is quite diversified here as there might be young photographers, middle age women spending their afternoons in comfortable undresses and hipster crowd shopping for quirky accessories. Colorful background and wall graffiti attracts photographers for great photography sessions. Among the famous historical places in Singapore, Haji Lane is a place you shouldn’t miss. The Kampong Glam District is the present day home of the Native Malay and Muslim Community that is centered with the picturesque Masjid Sultan. This place also has vintage record shops and cozy cafes for tourists to have a peaceful break in this busy lane. This lane is every fashionista’s paradise as it has beautiful dress shirts and an impressive retail stretch.




Sultan Mosque

One of the best Aingapore heritage buildings, this magnificent mosque was built in 1824 by the first sultan of Singapore named Sultan Hussein Shah. It has massive golden domes and huge prayer halls which can accommodate 5000 people for mass prayer. Sultan mosque has great religious importance and also is the biggest mosque in Singapore which makes it an important historical monument. It was renovated a century later it was built and donations by poor Muslims were made so that everyone can contribute their bit for reconstruction. Though this massive monument is a great attraction, tourists should also make some time to walk around the Arab Quarters where this mosque is located. An amalgamation of old and new shops selling food, clothing, souvenirs and many things are available in this street. Visitors are welcomed freely in this mosque if they are dressed appropriately, if not coats are provided.
